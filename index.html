Subtheme: Speculation

Overall Analysis Questions

  1. How has the proportion of investor purchases in Boston changed over time, and do any trends emerge around fluctuations in the economy?

    The reading, "Homes for Profit: An Analysis of Investor Activity in the Greater Boston Residential Real Estate Market, 2000 - 2022", discusses that investor purchases surged during periods of recession when housing prices were relatively lower, so I hope to explore whether the activity of investors can be visualized according to trends in economic fluctation.

  2. Where in Boston has investor activity been most concentrated, and how does it affect neighborhood demographics and housing affordability?

    I had assumed investor activity would be primarily in high-end apartments before reading "Homes for Profit: An Analysis of Investor Activity in the Greater Boston Residential Real Estate Market, 2000 - 2022", where I learned the most investor activity occurs in high density, low housing price submarkets, so I'm interested in how investor purchases heighten displacement pressures and change neighborhood demographics.

  3. What patterns emerge in the frequency, location, and profitability of flipped properties in Boston, and do they suggest new trends in housing speculation?

    Identifying emerging hotspots for flips and their profitability could provide valuable insights for policymakers, housing advocates, and prospective homebuyers trying to navigate Boston’s competitive market.
